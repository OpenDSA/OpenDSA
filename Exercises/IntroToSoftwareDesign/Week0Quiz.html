If I've already been reported for suspected cheating on one assignment, I should not worry about cheating on others ... after all, I can only get one F* for the course, right?

False
  • True

Each assignment is typically reported as a separate violation. While students typically receive an F* for their first violation, the second offense normally results in expulsion (permanent dismissal) from the university, meaning that you will be removed as a student and will not be allowed to enroll again (or finish your degree).

If I'm caught cheating, I will be notified:

When the instructor detects the violation, or when the Office of Academic Integrity begins processing the report
  • Before the next assignment
  • Within 2 weeks of the assignment
  • Immediately

Notification of students is at the discretion of the instructor. It may occur when the instructor reports the student, or it may happen later, once the Office of Academic Integrity contacts the student to gather more information. This may be long after the assignment is complete (in some cases, after the semester is over).

The typical penalty a student will receive for cheating on a programming assignment or a lab assignment is:

They will receive a grade of zero on the assignment
  • They will receive a "double-weighted" zero on the assignment (a zero that counts twice as much in their final grade calculation)
  • They will receive an F in the course
  • The instructor can choose what grade they will receive on the assignment, and in the course
  • They will receive an F* grade (where the asterisk denotes it is due to academic misconduct)
